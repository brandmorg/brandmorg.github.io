"Someone else always has to carry on the story..."

- Bilbo Baggins


The Lord of the Rings played a huge role in my life growing up. Whenever things were at the darkest for me, I would pick up the book and find myself once more in Middle Earth. There is a part in the story where Sam, when in Mordor, despite being overcome by weariness and losing hope, sees a star...

"There, peeping among the cloud-wrack above a dark tower high up in the mountains, Sam saw a white star twinkle for a while. The beauty of it smote his heart, as he looked up out of the forsaken land, and hope returned to him. For like a shaft, clear and cold, the thought pierced him that in the end the Shadow was only a small and passing thing: there was light and high beauty for ever beyond its reach. His song in the Tower had been defiance rather than hope; for then he was thinking of himself. Now, for a moment, his own fate, and even his master's ceased to trouble him."

Lord of the Rings has, and always will be, that star for me. No matter what I am going through in my life, no matter what Shadow I find rising in the East, the ability to open the book and fall once more into the Shire - amongst the Hobbit-folk and enjoying a pint - will always be what grants me the courage to face the things that I am afraid of and revitalizes my hope that it is temporary.


I would love to hear what Lord of the Rings means to you - if you're comfortable with sharing. I can't wait to read your stories.
